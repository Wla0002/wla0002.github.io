What Led Me Here?

College

Coming out of high school, like many others, I lacked a sense of direction. I decided to major in Industrial Engineering due to the fact that it is a very broad subject and can lead to many things.

I started in the lowest math class the Engineering program allowed. I was projected to graduate is 6.5 years.

X,Y, Input Output

During my battles with the curriculum I received a strong base in statistics, simulation, calculus, hypothesis testing, operations research, and programming with python. Some of my favorite classes were ones that gave me the tools to allow my ideas to come to fruition. Such as programming and statistical analysis. The curriculum helped me to understand the world as inputs(X’s) and outputs(Y’s).

Senior Year

I ended up taking summer classes three years in a row and cut 2 years off of my graduation date. My senior year I took an engineering elective that covered data querying with SQL, Pandas, Data Cleaning, Visualization with matplotlib, seaborn, and Tableau, all things that we know now to be fundamentals of Data Science. I concurrently had a semester long project where I had to analyze data from a fictional coffee company called sigma brew and diagnose their issues and recommend actions. This is where I began to really enjoy this stuff, I used many things like linear regression to find correlation between variables, various hypothesis testing methods, distributions, and I was using the things I was learning in the Data Science course to query and visualize the data that I was given. I use this experience as a reference to where my interest in Data Science became apparent and unpardonable.

Westinghouse Nuclear Internship

I had one more semester of college left after my summer internship at Westinghouse. I am not at liberty to share the specifics of my experience at Westinghouse, but I can say I was given free rein to create my own program that would benefit the company to streamline production. I was able to work independently and figure out how to use many different things in order to bring this product into fruition. Using many data science tools I was able to create a dashboard that the production planners and operators of machines could use to make better, statistics based decisions. Fortunately this didn’t quench my unwavering aspirations, and during development, I had brainstormed well beyond my own personal knowledge and capability. Leading to my discovery and eventual fascination with things concepts like Machine Learning, AI, and all of the possibilities that they can enable. There was some problems though, I had no earthly Idea how to utilize any of these things. However my curriculum at WVU provided me with a strong background in the math and statistics behind them.

This was very frustrating for me because I was unable to utilize these tools, this caused me to want to learn more about these things so would not be limited by my own knowledge.

What I’ve Been Doing

While learning about machine learning during my internship after being encouraged by people who use it at Westinghouse I enrolled at codecademy to learn more about it. During my final semester between classes I would go on to learn the more computer science side of the area that I lacked in. And after a brief break after I had graduated I enrolled in the Data Science Machine Learning Specialist Professional Course that codecademy offered. Here I had done the following:

  • relearned python (to further cement the language in my mind)
  • practiced querying with SQL, and Pandas
  • learned the standard practice for visualization
  • reviewed hypothesis testing and statistical distributions
  • learned about Exploratory Data Analysis
  • learned about webscraping with beautiful soup
  • learned about regex
  • learned the standard practice on how to communicate data science findings effectively

Currently I am finally on to learning about the machine learning specialization that I discovered while at Westinghouse. All the while creating my own personal portfolio of projects that highlight my abilities with data science techniques.

Where I plan on Going

From here on out I plan on continuing to fill my portfolio with data science projects, both prompted by codecademy and myself. I will continue to update this website with items and concepts that I’ve learned in order to paint a full picture of my skillset.
